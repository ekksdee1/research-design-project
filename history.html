The History of Typography

When you think of typography, you may think that it is only part of websites, and how we choose the fonts we choose, but that is scarcely the case. For example, what about newspapers and periodicals in the industrial revolution?

When talking about the history of typography, we can look back even further than just the industrial revolution, all the way back to 3000 BC! Ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs could be considered a form of typography, becuause the Egyptians chose how to make them look and how to arrange them, almost like a font by today's standards.

Then, we can go foward in history, to the mid-15th century where the introduction of the printing press was something new. The printing press was a means of spreading news to the general public, and as such the text had to be organized in a readable and logical fasion. Enter typography.

Skipping foward a bit, today we use it for not only newspapers, books, periodicals, but also websites! There are millions if not billions of websites on the web, and the majority of them have some kind of text for the user to read. What about how company logos look? For example, a popular car brand, Honda, uses a serif-type of font for the text part of their logo, "Honda". Or what about a popular fast food chain? Chick-Fil-A uses a decorative type font. How that text looks, how it's organized, and how it reads is typography at work.
